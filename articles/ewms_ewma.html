Context

Given a floating-point array $\boldsymbol{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ and a corresponding array of integer epoch timestamps $\boldsymbol{t} \in \mathbb{Z}^n$, we can compute an exponentially weighted/decayed moving sum (EWMS) or moving average (EWMA) of $\boldsymbol{x}$ using a single-pass algorithm. These are common time series operations useful for "smoothing" noisy observations or isolating trends in data; and the single-pass algorithms implemented in this article are performant for large datasets and can be applied to live data streams without needing to store previous observations in memory.
