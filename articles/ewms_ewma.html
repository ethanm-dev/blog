Context

Given a floating-point array $\boldsymbol{x} \in \mathbb{R}^N$ and a corresponding array of non-decreasing, integer, epoch timestamps $\boldsymbol{t} \in \mathbb{Z}^N$, we can compute an exponentially weighted/decayed moving sum (EWMS) or moving average (EWMA) of $\boldsymbol{x}$ using a single-pass algorithm. These are common time series operations useful for "smoothing" noisy observations or isolating trends in data; and the single-pass algorithms implemented in this article are performant for large datasets and can be applied to live data streams without needing to store previous observations in memory.

EWMS Derivation

Given an elapsed time of $t$ seconds and a half life of $\tau$ seconds, the exponential decay factor is defined as $$\alpha \> = \> 2^{-\frac{t}{\tau}} \in (0,1],$$ which equals 1 when $t = 0$ (no decay has occurred), equals $\frac{1}{2}$ when $t = \tau$ (hence the name half life), and approaches 0 as $t \rightarrow \infty$. Hence, for some index $n \in [1, N]$, the exponentially weighted moving sum of the first $n$ observations of $\boldsymbol{x}$ with half life $\tau$ is $$\rho_n \> = \> \sum_{i=1}^n w_{i,n} x_i$$ with decay weights $$w_{i,n} \> = \> 2^{-\frac{t_n - t_i}{\tau}}.$$

To define a recurrence relation for $\rho_n$ in terms of $\rho_{n-1}$, see that $$\begin{aligned} \rho_n \> &= \> \sum_{i=1}^n w_{i,n} x_i \\ &= \> w_{n,n} x_n + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} w_{i,n} x_i \\ &= \> 2^{-\frac{t_n - t_n}{\tau}} x_n + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{-\frac{t_n - t_i}{\tau}} x_i \\ &= \> 2^{-\frac{0}{\tau}} x_n + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{-\frac{(t_n - t_{n-1}) + (t_{n-1} - t_i)}{\tau}} x_i \\ &= \> x_n + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{\left( - \frac{t_n - t_{n-1}}{\tau} - \frac{t_{n-1} - t_i}{\tau}\right)} x_i \\ &= \> x_n + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{-\frac{t_n - t_{n-1}}{\tau}} 2^{-\frac{t_{n-1} - t_i}{\tau}} x_i \\ &= \> x_n + 2^{-\frac{t_n - t_{n-1}}{\tau}} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{-\frac{t_{n-1} - t_i}{\tau}} x_i \\ &= \> x_n + \alpha_n \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} w_{i,n-1} x_i \\ &= \> x_n + \alpha_n \rho_{n-1}, \end{aligned}$$ where $\alpha_n$ is the decay factor given the elapsed time $t_n - t_{n-1}$ seconds from the $n^{\text{th}}$ to the $(n-1)^{\text{th}}$ observation.

EWMA Derivation

For some index $n \in [1, N]$, the exponentially weighted moving average of the first $n$ observations of $\boldsymbol{x}$ with half life $\tau$ is $$\mu_n \> = \> \frac{\rho_n}{W_n} \> = \> \frac{\sum_{i=1}^n w_{i,n} x_i}{\sum_{i=1}^n w_{i,n}},$$ with the weight sum (using the same decay weights as the EWMS) $$W_n \> = \> \sum_{i=1}^n w_{i,n} \> = \> \sum_{i=1}^n 2^{-\frac{t_n - t_i}{\tau}}.$$ Put simply, the EWMA of a sequence is the EWMS divided by the sum of the decay weights.

Similarly to the EWMS derivation, in order to find a recurrence relation for $\mu_n$ in terms of $\mu_{n-1}$, start with the definition $$\begin{aligned} \mu_n \> &= \> \frac{\sum_{i=1}^n w_{i,n} x_i}{\sum_{i=1}^n w_{i,n}} \\ &= \> \frac{w_{n,n} x_n + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} w_{i,n} x_i}{w_{n,n} + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} w_{i,n}} \\ &= \> \frac{x_n + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{-\frac{(t_n - t_{n-1}) + (t_{n-1} - t_i)}{\tau}} x_i} {1 + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{-\frac{(t_n - t_{n-1}) + (t_{n-1} - t_i)}{\tau}}} \\ &= \> \frac{x_n + 2^{-\frac{t_n - t_{n-1}}{\tau}} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{-\frac{t_{n-1} - t_i}{\tau}} x_i} {1 + 2^{-\frac{t_n - t_{n-1}}{\tau}} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} 2^{-\frac{t_{n-1} - t_i}{\tau}}} \\ &= \> \frac{x_n + \alpha_n \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} w_{i,n-1} x_i}{1 + \alpha_n \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} w_{i,n-1}} \\ &= \> \frac{x_n + \alpha_n \rho_{n-1}}{1 + \alpha_n W_{n-1}} \\ &= \> \frac{x_n + \alpha_n W_{n-1} \mu_{n-1}}{1 + \alpha_n W_{n-1}} \\ &= \> \frac{x_n + (W_n - 1) \mu_{n-1}}{W_n}, \end{aligned}$$ which implies that the weight sum update rule is $$W_n \> = \> 1 + \alpha_n W_{n-1},$$ following which the EWMA $\mu_n$ can be determined using the previous EWMA $\mu_{n-1}$, the weight sum $W_n$ and the current observation $x_n$.
